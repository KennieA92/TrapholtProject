Arne Jacobsen Kubeflex

Permanent Exhibition

The house is called Kubeflex, as it consists of cube-shaped modules that can be flexibly assembled in countless ways and resized as needed.

Arne Jacobsen designed Kubeflex in 1969-70, the year before he died in 1971, but it was never put into production. Until 2002, the Jacobsen family used the house as a summer home, and since 2005 it has belonged to Trapholt. The house is the only one of its kind and is decorated with Arne Jacobsen design

Cafe Gustav Lind

The experience of Trapholt continues naturally when you step into Gustav Lind, which is Trapholt’s own little gastronomic experience.

Here the focus is on the sustainable, the green and of course ecology. The menu changes with the Danish season, and you are always guaranteed a good dining experience. Of course, there is also room for the little moment where you can enjoy a glass of wine or Mrs. Lind’s layer cake with accompanying coffee.

Gustav Lind Trapholt is an offer for the museum’s guests and can therefore only be visited by paid admission to Trapholt.
